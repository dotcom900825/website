Assignment 3, Example 2

Point of View

International Students often do not feel fully supported by the collegiate community. Although they are a large part of the student population, their circumstances often leave them at a social and academic disconnect. While there are various resources targeted for the international community, access to these organizations and opportunities are limited to those who know how to navigate through UCSD websites and search for this information. Thus, international students have trouble connecting with other students, utilizing academic resources, and familiarizing themselves with campus because of language and cultural barriers. We think that creating an app that will empower international students to use these opportunities on campus will allow them to become more actively involved on campus and in the larger community as a whole.

  2. Integration: We were inspired by the I-House community and the I-House website, because it focuses on the integration of I-House residents into the greater UCSD community. They host events in order to foster socialization between international and non-international students.

  3. Global: We were inspired by the possibility to exchange different cultures amongst students and to create an environment where international students can not only learn about other foreign cultures, but can learn about American culture as well.

  4. Community: This website is for the Multicultural Greek Council. We were inspired by this because multicultural fraternities and sororities help foster a community where students can connect with other students from the same cultural background.

  5. Enjoyment: Not only do we want international students to feel academically integrated, we want international students to feel welcome and enjoy the benefits of being at UCSD. And so, we were inspired by this picture of Spirit Night, because it represent students coming together and enjoying a UCSD experience/sporting event.

Prototypes


Prototype 1

The first prototype is an app that allows international students to first create a profile where they input where they're from, the classes they're taking, their interests, etc. Then, users access language resources, academic resources, and social calendars.

Here, the user can find a tutor that can speak their language or other international students in their class.

The user can also access language resources, UCSD news and calendars, information about I-House events, and a map.


Prototype 2

The second prototype, called Lost in Translation, is an app that allows international students to execute four important functions: a slang dictionary, a way to find tutors and language conversation tables, a UCSD calendar, and a map.

Here a drop down menu appears with information about the upcoming language conversation tables.

A drop down menu appears for international students to find tutors in their class.

Users can also access a calendar with both academic and social events.

A pop up appears with more detailed information about the events of the day.

Users can also access a map of UCSD to help navigate their way around campus.
